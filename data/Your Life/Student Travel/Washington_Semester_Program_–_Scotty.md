**Carnegie Mellon University Washington Semester Program (CMU/WSP)**

From embassy headquarters to nongovernmental organizations, think tanks to advocacy organizations, and consulting firms to media outlets, Washington, DC, is a focal point for many international and public policy activities.

Undergraduates from any course of study who would value firsthand policy experience are invited to apply to the [Carnegie Mellon University Washington Semester Program (CMU/WSP)](https://www.cmu.edu/ips/cmuwsp/), sponsored by the university’s Institute for Politics and Strategy. In this semester-long program, students live, work, and study in Washington, DC, coming into direct contact with political, business, and community leaders and learning about the most pressing policy issues of the day.

#### Frequently Asked Questions for CMUQ Students Participating in CMU/WSP:

**What year do I have to be to participate in CMU/WSP?**

CMU/WSP is intended for students in their junior year at CMUQ although second semester sophomores or first semester seniors may also be considered.

**What is the application process for CMU/WSP?** 

Submit the following documents to Emily Half, Deputy Director at CMU/WSP, at ehalf@andrew.cmu.edu:

-   Online application
-   One-page essay
-   Current résumé
-   Academic Advisor approval
-   Unofficial transcript
-   Two (2) letters of recommendation

For questions about the application process, please contact Emily Half, Deputy Director at CMU/WSP, at ehalf@andrew.cmu.edu.

**What is the deadline to apply for CMU/WSP?**

**March 1** for Fall semester and **October 1** for Spring semester.

**I’ll need a visa to study in the United States. What kind of visa do I need?** 

Students participating in CMU/WSP will need a J-1 visa. The Office of International Education in Pittsburgh will help coordinate the visa process.

**What kind of health insurance coverage do I need to participate in CMU/WSP?**

Carnegie Mellon University’s Student Health Insurance is available to students enrolled in CMU/WSP, so CMUQ students can enroll in Pittsburgh’s Aetna student health insurance during their time in D.C. for an additional charge.

**What kind of classes will I take during CMU/WSP?**

CMU/WSP students earn 48 units for their semester in Washington, interning approximately twenty-four hours per week in any sector or field of interest within Washington, DC, while taking classes three to four afternoons/evenings per week. Courses are taught by Carnegie Mellon faculty.

**Can I earn a minor in Politics and Public Policy by participating in CMU/WSP?**

-   If students wish to pursue a minor in _Politics and Public Policy_, students will need to complete _73-102 Principles of Microeconomics_and an additional course (from GUQ). Pre-approved course options are:  -   [US Political Systems – GOVT 020](https://myaccess.georgetown.edu/pls/bninbp/bwckschd.p_disp_detail_sched?term_in=201910&crn_in=36548) (typically offered in spring)
      -   [Comparative Political Systems – GOVT 040](https://myaccess.georgetown.edu/pls/bninbp/bwckschd.p_disp_detail_sched?term_in=201910&crn_in=32659) (typically offered in spring)
      -   [International Relations GOVT 060](https://myaccess.georgetown.edu/pls/bninbp/bwckschd.p_disp_detail_sched?term_in=201910&crn_in=32657) (typically offered in fall)
