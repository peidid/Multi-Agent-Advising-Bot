##STUDENT ORGANIZATION CONSTITUTION CHECKLIST			       -

This constitution checklist is created to help Carnegie Mellon University in Qatar student organizations define organization framework and substance, manage organization operations, and maintain institutional knowledge.

ORGANIZATION NAME AND LOGO
Name of the organization, and any abbreviated name or acronym if it intends to use one.
Logo of the organization.
(if applicable) Email address of the organization.

MISSION AND GOALS
Mission statement that is clear, precise, and definitive (generally 60 words or less). Anyone who reads the mission should understand what the organization is about and why it is in existence.
Philosophy, goals, and/or purpose for which the organization has been created. For Example:
What is the purpose for establishing the organization?
What is the target student group of the organization?
What services will be provided and for whom?
What is the intended impact of the organization on the University community?
Following sentence in its own paragraph: “All activities and functions of the organization must be legal under University, local, state, and federal laws”.

MEMBERSHIP
Define if the student organization will have an exclusive membership.
Generally, most CMU-Q student organizations may not have an exclusive membership where non-member students cannot participate in the organization’s activities. However, certain organizations (e.g. competitive sports teams) may have activities restricted to members (e.g. training sessions and tournaments), with corresponding selection processes (e.g. tryouts).
If yes, define recruitment period, qualification, duration, privileges, responsibilities, and withdrawal or removal processes of membership.
Non-discrimination statement for the organization’s commitment to abide by the CMU Statement of Assurance MUST be included.
Non-hazing statement: “Hazing will not be used as a condition of membership in this organization”.

OFFICERS
Officer titles and organizational structure.
Non-sports student organizations are required to define at least 3 leadership positions.
Officer descriptions.
List the duties and responsibilities of each officer.
Who is eligible for office (necessary experience, class level requirements)?
Statement on whether an officer can hold more than one position concurrently. If allowed, explain circumstances.
Will there be any type of executive committee including officers?
Statement on voting rights of officers. For example: president only votes in the event of a tie.
Terms of Office.
Specify time frame (e.g. semester, academic year, etc.)
When do officers assume positions?

ELECTION/SELECTION OF OFFICERS
Officer selection must take place at least once per academic year.
Nomination procedure if utilizing elections, unless your organization has an application and/or interview process.
How are nominations made?
Are there self-nominations?
Election/selection process
Describe the election process. For example: “Officers of this organization shall be elected by a majority vote (51%) of the regular student membership of the organization”.
When, where?
(if applicable) Order of elections, order of speeches,
How the votes are to be cast, counted (secret ballot, hands, etc.)
What number or percentage of members or quorum is necessary for elections to be valid? (i.e. 2/3’s, etc.)
Who is eligible to vote?
What is the process for notifying others or posting information regarding organization elections?
(if applicable) Are letters, flyers, or posters used on campus?
Are verbal announcements made at meetings?
How long is the notification and posting period?
(if applicable) Statement on absentee ballots or proxy ballots for nominations AND elections.
(if applicable) Statement on the run-off process.
(if applicable) Statement that addresses the issue of a tie.
When newly selected officers shall take office.

OFFICER VACANCIES
Whether officers can be re-elected (if so, explain).
Procedure for Removal of Officers.
Who can initiate the removal of an officer?
What is the specific procedure to be followed for removal?
What type of vote is required for removal?
Procedure for Filling Vacated Offices.
Are special elections held?
Is ascending order used?
Are appointments made, or are interviews coordinated?
Appeal Policy.
Is there such a policy in your organization?
Is there a set time period to carry out an appeal?
What are the steps involved with an appeal?

MEETINGS
Types of meetings (e.g. Officer and Membership meetings). Including when/how often they occur, who attends, and (if applicable) who can vote in each.
Quorum requirements to officially conduct and approve business of the organization (at least a majority of members/officers).
How and by whom meetings can be called and what kind of notice is required.
(if applicable) What procedures will be used to conduct meetings (i.e. Robert’s Rules of Order).

(if applicable) COMMITTEES
Discuss any standing and special committees (formation, selection, powers, membership, chairperson selection, and duties).
Discuss the roles and responsibilities of committee chairpersons.

FINANCE AND RECORDS
Spending authorization.
Which members have authority to spend the organization's money?
(if applicable) What processes and records are required for spendings?
Limitation on how funds may be spent. Example: “Organizational funds may be spent on items such as office supplies, events/activities, publicity, travel expenses, and conference fees, but will not be used for anything illegal under University, local, state, and federal laws”.
Financial records and institutional knowledge documentation.
What records and documentation will club officers maintain during club cycles?
Transition documents and procedure from outgoing to incoming officers.

ADVISOR
Define if the student organization would like to work with and receive mentorship from an advisor.
Academic and Career student organizations are required to identify and work with an advisor.
Other clubs are strongly encouraged to identify and work with an advisor.
More details see Student Organization Advisor Agreement.
If yes, what are the role, responsibility, duties, and authority of the advisor?
Students’ responsibilities to the advisor.
Nomination and selection process for advisors.
Length of term of advisor (can be for an unspecified amount of time).
Process by which an advisor can be removed.

RATIFICATION AND EMPOWERMENT
Necessary approval needed for approval of the constitution.
State when the constitution will take effect.

AMENDMENTS
Proposing Amendments
What is the procedure for proposing amendments (i.e. in writing, verbally, etc.)?
Who can propose amendments?
What is the timeline for reading and voting?
Provisions
When can an amendment be proposed?
Is there any notice required in advance for amending the constitution and/or by-laws?
How many members must be present for adoption of the amendment?
Who can vote on the amendment, and how many votes are required for passage?
When do new amendments take effect?
Describe the process for amending the constitution. For example: “This constitution and by-laws may be amended by a 2/3 vote of the entire voting student membership”.

HISTORY
Must include the date of creation.
Must include all dates of revision.
