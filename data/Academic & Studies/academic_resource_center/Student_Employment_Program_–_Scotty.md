The Academic Resource Center is a great place to work, learn, and earn while you attend college. Getting a job while at college can provide you the extra money you need to meet college expenses while providing you with opportunities to give back to our students via tutoring and offering learning support for various courses. The ARC hires over 90 students every semester in various roles like Course Assistants and non-academic positions.

Who is a Course Assistant?

Course Assistants (CA) are students who work with faculty and the ARC to support student learning at Carnegie Mellon Qatar.

Course Assistants help faculty members with specific courses. Responsibilities vary, but usually include grading, assisting in class, leading recitations, tutoring, and occasionally proctoring exams.

Why apply for a Course Assistant job?

Serving as a Course Assistant is an excellent opportunity to:

-   Help other students learn
-   Deepen your own understanding of a subject you like
-   Improve your communication and teaching skills
-   Work closely with professors and academic staff
-   Earn the highest pay rate on campus

Am I qualified to become a Course Assistant?

To serve as a Course Assistant, you need to qualify for the following criteria:

-   An A or B in the course
-   A 3.0 or above cumulative QPA
-   A clean record of academic integrity and no Community Standards warning letters issued for **at least the past two consecutive semesters** (i.e., no recent infractions) at the time of employment.
-   Strong interpersonal skills and a sense of responsibility
-   Completion of the ARC’s new CA training program during the first week of the semester

What is a Non-Academic job?

Non-Academic jobs are positions that require basic skills acquired through some previous work experience, training and/or completion of related coursework. Responsibilities may require applying basic organizational and/or more technical knowledge, operation and accountability for more complex equipment or performing routine tasks. Departments that have non-academic job opportunities are usually the Library, IT, Admissions, Student Affairs etc.

How many hours may I work?

Students may work a total of 20 hours per week during the Fall and Spring semesters and 37.5 hours per week during the Summer and Winter breaks. You should physically be present in the State of Qatar to be eligible for the Student Employment program.

What are the hourly pay rates for student workers?

The non-academic Student Assistants will be paid 36.5 QAR per hour, Course Assistants at 47.5 QAR per hour, Supplemental Instruction(SI) Leaders at 50 QAR per hour and Grader level at 40 QAR per hour.

What do I do if I need a letter of recommendation or employment verification of my CA position for another job or internship?

Employment verification requests should be directed to the ARC Director. Letters of recommendation should be requested from your faculty supervisor for your CA job.
